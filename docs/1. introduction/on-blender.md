### Forensic Applications

Blender's comprehensive toolset makes it particularly valuable for visual investigation work. It excels at crime scene reconstruction, allowing investigators to create accurate 3D models of locations and events. The software is equally effective for accident analysis, enabling precise spatial relationships and trajectory calculations. Beyond reconstruction, Blender supports architectural forensics for building analysis, evidence visualization to present complex findings clearly, timeline animations to demonstrate sequences of events, and spatial analysis to understand relationships between objects and locations. These capabilities make Blender an essential tool for professionals working in investigative and forensic contexts.

## Shortcuts

Blender is designed with the use of shortcuts in mind. Practically everything has a shortcut for it. Even experienced users discover, after years of use, a hidden shortcuts they weren't aware of. Fortunately, the team at [CGBoost.com](http://cgboost.com/) has created a handy document listing the most useful ones. You can download, print, and reference it when you're searching for a specific key or simply browse through to familiarize yourself with what's available.

## Add-ons & Extensions

Blender has an extensive ecosystem of add-ons (or extensions, as they have been recently dubbed). This offers a wealth of customization options. Whether you're looking to streamline your workflow, add a new feature, or a handle specific task, if you think that there should be an add-on for it, there likely is one. However, not all add-ons are created equal. Some may require additional configuration or troubleshooting, and others may only work with specific Blender versions. Additionally, some add-ons are actively maintained, while others may have been abandoned.
